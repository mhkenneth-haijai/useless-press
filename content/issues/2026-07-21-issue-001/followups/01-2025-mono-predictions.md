title: "What We Got Wrong Last Year"
deck: "Twelve months ago, we made three predictions about monochrome compact cameras. Two of them have aged poorly."
---

Twelve months ago, in a private note we keep meaning to publish, we predicted:

1. **Fujifilm would announce a monochrome X-Pro by mid-2026.** *Unconfirmed.* As of this writing, Fuji Rumors still publishes quarterly items titled "Where is the monochrome Fuji?" The camera has not appeared.
2. **Sony would not enter the dedicated-monochrome market in 2026.** *Confirmed correct.* Sony continues to issue high-resolution colour sensors and let B&W live in the Sony A7R V JPEG menu.
3. **Someone would build a monochrome compact camera under $1,500 by end of 2026.** *Probably wrong.* The Ricoh GR IV Monochrome, the natural candidate, is $2,199. The Pentax K-3 III Mono, the other candidate, hovers near $2,400. The under-$1,500 monochrome compact remains hypothetical.

So much for predictions. We will check in again at the end of 2026.
